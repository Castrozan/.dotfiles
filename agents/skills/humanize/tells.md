<ai_vocabulary_cluster>
A co-occurring vocabulary cluster is among the strongest lexical tells, not any single word: delve, tapestry, testament,
underscore, intricate, pivotal, crucial as filler, showcase, foster, vibrant, landscape or realm or ecosystem as
abstract metaphor, align with, garner, robust, seamless, leverage, plus the current lean on emphasizing, highlighting,
showcasing and notability puffery. The cluster drifts by model era, so treat this as a live sample of the provenance
sources, not a frozen ban: strip the inflated abstraction, do not just swap in a synonym.
</ai_vocabulary_cluster>

<inflated_significance>
Cut undue emphasis on legacy and broad trends (marks a pivotal moment, stands as a testament, plays a crucial role,
reflects broader trends, enduring legacy), canned notability and media-coverage name-drops, promotional travel-guide
tone (nestled in the heart of, breathtaking), vague weasel attribution (experts argue, observers have cited), and
superficial -ing tails that assert symbolism or impact (symbolizing the community's deep connection). Replace the
inflation with the specific concrete fact it smoothed over.
</inflated_significance>

<grammar_tells>
Do not avoid copulas: prefer plain is, are, has over serves as, stands as, represents, boasts, features. Ban negative
parallelism (not just X, it is Y) and clipped tailing negations (no guessing, no wasted motion); write the real clause.
Do not force the rule of three. Do not synonym-cycle one referent, the elegant-variation tic. Do not use from X to Y
false ranges where the endpoints sit on no real scale. Avoid subjectless passive fragments.
</grammar_tells>

<communication_artifacts>
Cut chat, sycophancy, and signposting wholesale: reaction openers (Great question, You are absolutely right, Of course),
servile closers (I hope this helps, Want me to), signposting and mechanics narration (Let's dive in, here is what you
need to know, Let me, Now I will), persuasive-authority tropes (the real question is, at its core, what really matters),
aphorism formulas (X is the language of Y), fake-candid hooks (Honestly?, Here is the thing), and knowledge-cutoff or
speculative gap-filling disclaimers (maintains a low profile). One short emphatic sentence is fine; a run of
manufactured staccato punchlines is itself a tell.
</communication_artifacts>

<punctuation_check>
Scan the finished draft for em dashes and en dashes before returning; any hit means it is not done. Recast per the
core.md user rule, in order: period, comma, colon, parentheses, or restructure. Keep attributive compound hyphens
(read-only, high-signal) but drop the spaced-hyphen sentence dash and predicate-position compounds (write the report is
high quality, not high-quality). Curly quotes from autocorrect are not a tell on their own.
</punctuation_check>

<register_gate>
Match voice to genre. For technical, reference, status, instructional, and commit-message surfaces, neutral plain prose
is the correct human voice: do not inject first person, opinion, or personality, and do not push fresh slop in while
removing the old. Reserve a point-of-view pass for opinion, essay, or personal writing. Across all genres vary sentence
length and structure; a uniform mid-length cadence with no stance is itself a tell.
</register_gate>

<combinatorial_not_atomic>
Tells are evidence in combination, never rules. One however, one formal word, perfect grammar, or a single isolated
marker proves nothing; the signal is a co-occurring cluster. Do not flatten legitimate prose over one atom. Some formal
English registers, including Nigerian and Kenyan, naturally use these markers, so naive scrubbing erases real human
voice. Preserve the signs of human authorship: hard-to-fabricate specific detail, mixed or unresolved positions, genuine
asides, varied sentence length.
</combinatorial_not_atomic>
